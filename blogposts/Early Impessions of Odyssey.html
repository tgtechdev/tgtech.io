Early Impressions of AC Odyssey

I started playing Assassins Creed Odyssey on the Sony PS5 a few days ago. Here are my first initial thoughts - I'll start with the bad.

The Good

The interesting story about ancient Greece and the war between Sparta and Athens. The cool and unique sets of amour. Say you have a better set of amour in your inventory (but you don't like the way it looks), you can equip it and change the way it looks to a better looking set that you like. The ancient city of Athens and the historical characters in it, and the way of its design. You can destroy Sparta's or Athens' hold on a certain territory and you can fight to give it to the opposing side, or defend it. I also think that the mercenary system is cool but it's also pretty repetitive after a while because there are new mercenaries that replace the old ones. I like the opening scene of king Leonidas' fighting the Persians with the 300 Spartan troops.

The Bad

It's an Assassins Creed game. But 10 hours in and I have seen nothing relating with the assassins. The leveling and RPG system is weird - say you are only interested in the story, you can't progress through the play because of the leveling system. Let's say you have to be level 20 to do another story quest, but you are only level 15, it'll take about a few hours of war missions and side quests just to get enough xp to level up to that. And the power levels are quite stupid for me to say the least. Ubisoft has confirmed that Kassandra is the cannon choice for the main character. I think is great that we get another female protagonist. But why then make the player have a choice between a non-cannon Alexios and a cannon Kassandra? It's just confusing for me quite frankly. Just make one character if you are going to do that.

What's Next?

I am currently progressing throughout the main story but I am also tracking down the cult of Kosmos and eliminating the mercenaries above me to rank up. I'll keep you in the loop with my progression.
