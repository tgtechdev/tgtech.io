Crusader Kings 3: First Impressions

What is the game

Crusader Kings 3 is a dark ages/medieval sandbox game where you can play as any ruler in the start dates or you can make your own ruler. There are things you have to take care of which are vassals, succession, heirs, alliances and your health. For this review, I am have been playing this game on a PlayStation 5.

Main story points of the gameplay

The main ways of playing CK3 are tall,wide and big. Each having there own different traits.

Tall: Only hold a duchy or kingdom or a certain amount of land and develop and advance it technologically as fast as you can, make it as rich as possible and then start to take more land.

Wide: Make or have a really powerful army then have or get loads of claims on kingdoms or empires then just conquer as much as you can.

Big: Take as much land as you can and develop it as much as you can.

Historical Accuracy

So the historical accuracy at the start is accurate but as the game goes on it really depends what the AI does. But it also depends what you want to do. And the start points of the game and end points will always be the same, the game will always start in 867 and end around 1450 or 1453.

Character development

So you start as a character you picked or you continue as your player heir. If you start as a man or a woman in their 30s or 40s their going to have multiple different skill points in different educations such as marshal, stewardship or diplomacy. but if your new character is young then they wont. Heirs: So heirs depend on your inheritance laws such as high partition or primogeniture or elections. and introducing these laws depends on your crown authority to change certain laws you need absolute crown authority. And your legitimacy depends a lot on your overall rule and how people respect you as a ruler. Say if you lost lots of land and all of the wars you faced you legitimacy would be 0 and there would be revolts and nobody would want to marry into your house.

How your realm is split depends on how many children you have and what their claims are and sometimes can inherit your wife/ husbands land if they die before you. As you character gets older they loose prowess and become infirm and also can get other negative old age traits.

Prowess is basically you characters strength, fighting skill, how good they are as a knight and their overall muscle mass. This can be altered with certain house lifestyle traits. Your culture matters in this game because some cultures are better suited for your play style or what you want to focus on. For example if you want good longbow men you should pick the welsh culture or just add it onto your own if you are your culture head. Alternatively, if you want to increase development, which will speed up your cultural fascination progress speed, it will increase how fast you advance and how much new technology you get and how fast you get it.

Favourite part so far

There is no singular part that i enjoy more than others, but there are some things i enjoy doing. like getting dynasty/house traits these traits affect everyone in your house and everyone related to you if they are not apart of another house. There are multiple different dynasty legacies to choose from such as law, war, blood and family and many more.

Each of the different legacies in the options to choose from can affect the entirety of how you and your family play. If you choose blood your family will have really good traits that will make their life/ rule easier. war will make you better at war, law will make your subjects/ vassals or liege like you more as well as the public in general and family can make you more likely to have heirs, make them like you more and also keep your prowess as you age.
